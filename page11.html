There are two GOVERNMENT PROGRAMS desinged To Help Us Pay For Our Long Term Care.

( NO! , THESE PROGRAMS ARE NOT JUST FOR POOR PEOPLE)

1. MEDICAID

Medicaid ICP, Institutional Care Program this is NOT welfare. This is a Skilled Nursing Care Benefit , and Medicaid Diversion or sometimes referred to as the Waiver program which when the financial need is required may be combined with the Veterans Pension with Aid and Attendance Benefit.

These programs are primarily most useful in circumstances requiring Assisted Living Care.

2. VETERANS PENSION with the AID and ATTENDANCE BENEFIT

No Veteran, the spouse of or surviving spouse of a veteran should live in poverty. while this benefit can be very useful for skilled Nursing Care is commonly used for Assisted Living Benefits.

         - Veteran: proof of service will be required.  

         - 65 year of age

         - 90 days consecutive service; 1 day during war time

         - Honorably Discharged

         - Spouse or Surviving Spouse
