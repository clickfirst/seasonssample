Take the Following Steps to Protect Your Assets and Help You Pay for Long Term Care

STEP 1

Our first step together will be a complimentary review of the current  Advanced Directives , that is the existing Power of Attorney, Health Care Surrogate and Living Will. This is important to make sure they are in compliance with the current State of Florida statutes and that they grant the necessary powers of authority, using specific language the POA , for this type of advanced planning. We also provide a complimentary consultation to explain and further educate the family on the various benefits available, what steps would be necessary to be taken to have a successful filing and result in an award of benefits. At no time are they under any obligation to retain our firm.

We can share with you with a reasonable degree of confidence that we can help to qualify most individuals  and their families to qualify for benefits regardless of Income and Assets . 

Tactically we will work with you and the nursing staff at to establish the required "level of care" ; once having established evidence as to the "need for  and the individual required level of care" we will then demonstrate the "financial need" for benefits, and strategically  engage in a process provide for Asset Preservation protecting the estate value from" spend down" and there by STRETECHING the available financial resources to provide for the best care and highest quality of life.


STEP 2

Thanks to modern technology we can provide you with some preliminary information  prior to our getting together.

Please pay particular attention to the attached Word Document  labeled “Required Items Check List for Government Benefits  Revised Color Coded”. These are the items of personal identification and documentation copies of which are required to be submitted along with the respective applications for benefits, have a successful review, result in a favorable decision and the timely award of benefits.

While there is some commonality, I have color coded those items that are necessary for each  respective filing: Yellow Highlights for the Medicaid Benefit , Blue Highlights for the Veterans Benefit. 

To make the most efficient use of our time together we ask the family to begin to try and locate these items . You most likely they  won't be able to find everything, but at least now they will have a check list and not inadvertently discard something that may be very difficult to replace when and if needed.  .  

As you may be aware applications for benefits are " time and date stamped" at submission ; therefore the filing process is time sensitive.


STEP 3

At the conclusion of our first meeting having reviewed your loved ones personal information, confirmed the  Income and available Assets, we will be able to provide you and your family with the following:

1) an opinion as to, Can they and your family actually benefit from hiring professional help or is seeking professional counsel unnecessary? Very much like Truth and Lending Disclosure 

2) a Financial "Transition Plan" so you will have a the frame work of a budget to work within until financial support begins to be received.

3) the Estimated Patient Responsibility or  Cost of Care; this is the amount of money your loved one will be responsible for paying “ out of pocket”  each month to Consulate Heath Care or an  ALF upon discharge.
